The Glass Sail Leviathan (Speculivelum abyssalis)

An active predator of the twilight zone, this creature is built for both speed in the dense, low-pressure deep and for gravity-defying aerial ballets at the surface. It is the closest this world has to a breaching whale, but its motives and methods are entirely alien.

Biology and Form:
The Leviathan is a streamlined, serpentine predator reaching lengths of over 300 meters. Its body is covered in overlapping, flexible plates of a transparent, chitin-like material, making it nearly invisible in the water column. It navigates and hunts using a sophisticated array of infrared-sensitive pits along its flanks, detecting the faint body heat of prey in the otherwise uniformly cold deep ocean.

Its most striking feature is a massive, retractable dorsal crest that can be unfurled. This "sail" is a lattice of cartilage and a photosensitive, chromatophore-rich membrane that can shift from perfect transparency to a blinding, mirror-like silver in an instant.

The Spectacle: The Sun-Dance
This creature's life cycle is intimately tied to the gas giant's long eclipse. After enduring the 4.3-hour total darkness when the moon is in its parent's shadow, the Leviathan rockets toward the surface just as the first rays of the sun re-emerge. Using powerful, undulating strokes of its body, it propels itself upward.

The breach is explosive. Two-thirds of its 300-meter body clears the surface, rising in a slow, majestic arc made possible by the 0.4g gravity. At the apex of its leap, it unfurls its colossal dorsal sail. The mirror-like membrane catches the returning sunlight, flashing a brilliant, star-like beacon visible for hundreds of kilometers across the ocean. It hangs in the air for a breathtaking 20-30 seconds, its transparent body glistening with cascading water, before crashing back down with a thunderous impact that can be felt by submersibles kilometers away. This "Sun-Dance" is hypothesized to be a method of shedding parasites, stunning surface prey, or a planet-wide, post-eclipse celebration of light.
